# Viewfinder: Data Science

Adapted from the original "[Viewfinder: AI Ethics Toolkit for Libraries & Archives](https://www.lib.montana.edu/responsible-ai/viewfinder/)," **Viewfinder: Data Science** is a participatory toolkit designed to facilitate ethical reflection about responsible AI implementation in a statistics and data science setting. This toolkit is specifically intended for use in a statistics or data science classroom, but can be also be used in alternative environments. Participants consider a variety of AI scenarios through the lens of various stakeholder perspectives, providing opportunities for reflection on values and potential ethical risks.

## How to Use the Toolkit
The toolkit is organized into three sections: **Scenarios**, **Stakeholders**, and **Values**. Follow these five steps to conduct an ethical reflection:

### Step 1: Choose Scenario (2 min)
Select one scenario card from the toolkit or draft your own AI implementation project or real-world scenario.

### Step 2: Your Values (5 min)
Consider which values are of concern to *you* regarding the scenario. Identify the **three values** that are of most concern from your personal or professional perspective.

### Step 3: Choose Stakeholder (2 min)
Choose a stakeholder card at random. This card should represent a perspective different from your own (e.g., a student, a donor, a vendor, or a community member).

### Step 4: Stakeholder Values (5 min)
Step into that stakeholder's shoes. Consider which values are of concern to *them*. Identify the **three values** that would be of most concern to that specific stakeholder.

### Step 5: Reflection Prompts (15 min)
Reflect on and respond to the provided Reflection Prompts. Record your notes and key insights in the provided worksheet.

> **Play Again:** Print your results and play again with a different stakeholder or scenario for deeper insights!
